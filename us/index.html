Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about studying in the USA

US universities typically require: Academic transcripts, English proficiency test scores (TOEFL/IELTS), Standardized test scores (SAT/GRE/GMAT), Statement of Purpose, Letters of Recommendation, and Financial documentation.

The F1 visa process typically takes 2-4 weeks. However, processing times can vary based on the embassy and time of year. We recommend applying as early as possible.

Optional Practical Training (OPT) allows F1 students to work in their field of study for up to 12 months after graduation. STEM graduates can extend this to 24 additional months, providing up to 3 years of work authorization.

Living costs vary by location. On average, students spend $20,000 per year on living expenses, including accommodation, food, transportation, and personal expenses.

F1 students can work on-campus for up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and 40 hours during breaks. Off-campus work requires authorization.
